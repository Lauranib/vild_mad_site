The taste

The green juniper berries have a delicate citrus flavor, while the blue, ripe berries lean more towards pine, dill, and mild pepper - they pop upon biting and have a slightly grainy texture.

The smell

Juniper has a turpentine-like, perfumed scent.

Risk of confusion

At a distance, juniper can easily be confused with thuja or cypress, which, however, do not have prickly needles but short, scale-like leaves that are closely attached to the stem.

Risotto with King Trumpets

Ingredients:

  • 1 onion, finely chopped

  • 1 clove of garlic, finely chopped

  • 400 g risotto rice

  • 300 g mushrooms

  • 1 liter vegetable broth

  • 50 g Parmesan cheese, freshly grated

  • 2 tablespoons olive oil

  • 1 tablespoon butter

  • Salt

  • Freshly ground black pepper

For serving:

  • 50 g Parmesan cheese, freshly grated

  • 1 handful of flat-leaf parsley, freshly chopped

Method:

  • Sauté onions and garlic in a pot with oil until the onions are translucent and soft. Add rice and stir well. Pour 1 dl of vegetable broth into the pot and stir until the rice has absorbed the liquid. Add more vegetable broth gradually until all the liquid is used, and the rice is tender.

  • Sear the mushrooms on a very hot pan with butter for 2 minutes and then stir them into the pot with risotto along with freshly grated Parmesan cheese. Season with salt and freshly ground black pepper.

  • Serve with grated Parmesan and parsley.
